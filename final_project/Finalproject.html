Research Question: ¶

What factors are the best predictors of driver race standings?

Introduction: ¶

Formula One (F1) is the world’s premier single-seater motorsport championship, governed by the FIA and contested by ten teams (twenty drivers) across a global calendar of Grands Prix. Each race weekend features practice sessions for setup, a high-stakes qualifying segment that sets the grid order, and the main race, where drivers compete over a fixed distance—earning points (25 for first down to 1 for tenth) toward both the Drivers’ and Constructors’ Championships. In this project were researching which factors best predict a driver’s race standing? We focus on four potential predictors: nationality, constructor, and driver age to assess their relevance to on-track performance. Understanding these relationships is important not only for teams aiming to optimize strategy and driver selection, but also for fans and analysts seeking deeper insights into the sport’s competitive dynamics. Our analysis shows that constructors are the most powerful predictor of race results; age and nationality exhibit more modest associations.

We merged the race results and constructor standings tables with a left join on raceId and constructorId, which kept every race result even if a constructor had no standing for that race.
